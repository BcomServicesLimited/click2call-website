What Are AI Voice Tools?

AI voice tools are software features that use artificial intelligence, machine learning, and natural language processing to extract value from spoken phone conversations. Rather than simply recording calls, they convert every conversation into structured, searchable data that can be reviewed, measured, and acted upon.

The process begins with call transcription, which converts speech to text in real time. From there, the AI applies additional layers of analysis: sentiment scoring rates the emotional tone of each call on a scale of 1 to 10, category tagging classifies the call topic automatically (for example, Callback Request, Account Management, or Technical Issue), and AI summaries condense a full conversation into a few sentences so managers can review calls in seconds rather than minutes.

Click2Call goes further than basic transcription. The platform includes an AI Receptionist that answers and routes calls by voice, an AI Speech generator for creating professional voice recordings from text, AI Voicemail with customisable AI-read greetings, AI Chat for text and voice-based agent interactions, and Transcription Alerts that email you when the AI detects key phrases, negative sentiment, or profanity in a call.

The AI Reports dashboard gives you a complete picture of your call activity over any date range. Run a report to see average sentiment scores by user, call volume broken down by category, average call length per agent, which names were spoken most often, and which businesses were mentioned in conversations.

AI Speech — Generate Voice Recordings from Text

AI Speech lets you create professional voice recordings without a recording studio or voice talent. Type your message, choose a voice actor, and click Generate. The recording is ready to use immediately as a hold message, IVR prompt, voicemail greeting, or welcome announcement.

A wide range of voice actors are available across both male and female voices, including Alice, Emma, Isabella, Lily, Annmarie, Gracie, Sofia, Asya, and more. Voices marked with an asterisk are recommended for conversational speech. You can add natural pauses to recordings using commas and full stops in your text.

This feature is particularly useful for businesses that need to update their on-hold messaging frequently, or for those setting up a new phone system who want a polished, professional sound from day one without the cost of a professional voice-over.

AI Receptionist

The AI Receptionist handles incoming calls by greeting the caller, understanding their request using voice recognition, and routing them to the appropriate department or extension — without any human involvement. It operates at all times, including outside business hours, and never puts a caller on hold to transfer them manually.

Setup is straightforward. You write a welcome message, choose an AI voice actor, and define which phone lines connect directly to the receptionist. You then create departments and describe what each one handles. When a caller explains their reason for calling, the AI matches their request to the right department and transfers the call automatically.

Additional controls include Replay Message (how many times to repeat the prompt if there is no response), Silence Timeout, Total Timeout, and toggles for Hold Message and Failure Message. These settings ensure callers always receive a professional experience even when the AI cannot identify their request.

AI Voicemail

AI Voicemail replaces static voicemail greetings with dynamically generated AI voice messages. You configure separate messages for when a line is unavailable and when it is busy. The placeholder MAILBOXNAME is automatically replaced with the name assigned to each extension, so every person on your team has a personalised greeting without recording anything individually.

Voicemail messages are also transcribed by the AI, so you can read a voicemail rather than listening to it — and search across all voicemails by keyword.

Transcription Alerts

Transcription Alerts send you an email notification whenever the AI detects a specific trigger in a call transcript. You can configure alerts for key phrases (for example, a competitor name or a compliance keyword), negative sentiment scores below a threshold, or profanity detected during a call.

This is particularly useful for compliance-sensitive industries, quality assurance teams, and managers who want to be notified of escalations without manually reviewing every call. Alerts are delivered in real time after the call ends and the transcript is processed.
