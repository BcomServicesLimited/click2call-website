Click2Call vs Telstra IP Telephony (TIPT)

The short version: both are cloud business phone systems, and on a like-for-like calling bundle they cost within about a dollar a month of each other. The real difference is commitment and control. Telstra IP Telephony (TIPT) is a licensed platform set up through a Telstra account representative, with a 12-month minimum term on licences, up to 36 months on rented equipment, and your own network to arrange separately. Click2Call is a self-serve Australian Cloud PBX at a flat $25 per user per month ex GST — no lock-in contract, no equipment to rent, no separate network project, and AI call recording and transcription included as standard.

Last updated: July 2026. Telstra figures below are drawn from Telstra's published TIPT Critical Information Summary (July 2026); confirm current Telstra pricing and terms with Telstra before deciding.

Key takeaways

  • No lock-in vs 12–36 months. Click2Call is month-to-month, cancel any time. TIPT licences have a 12-month minimum term and rented equipment up to 36 months, with an early termination charge to leave early.
  • Self-serve vs account-managed. Sign up for Click2Call online and be running in under an hour. TIPT is arranged through a Telstra Business Technology Centre and needs a compatible Telstra account.
  • No separate network project. Click2Call works over the internet you already have. TIPT requires you to arrange and reconfigure network access (IP VPN or internet) before the service can be delivered.
  • AI included. Click2Call includes call recording, AI transcription, AI summaries and an AI receptionist. A standard TIPT feature pack provides auto attendant, hunt group, voicemail and the Webex app.
  • Price is close. This isn't a "we're cheaper" page. A TIPT Standard licence with Complete Calling is about $26.40/month inc GST; a Click2Call user is about $27.50/month inc GST. Choose on flexibility, not a dollar.

When Telstra IP Telephony might suit you better

A fair comparison cuts both ways. Telstra IP Telephony can be the right choice if:

  • •Your team makes a very high volume of outbound calls — TIPT Complete Calling offers unlimited national and fixed-to-mobile calling, which can beat a per-minute model for heavy callers.
  • •You already run a Telstra IP VPN and want voice on the same managed network and single Telstra bill.
  • •You prefer a large-carrier account-managed relationship and have the internal resources to manage a longer-term contract and network setup.

If instead you want to be up and running today, keep your options open month to month, and skip the network project and equipment rental — that's where Click2Call fits.

Common questions

Is Click2Call cheaper than Telstra IP Telephony (TIPT)?
Not necessarily on the headline licence price. Per Telstra's TIPT Critical Information Summary (July 2026), a TIPT Standard user licence is $15.40/month inc GST, and adding TIPT Complete Calling for unlimited national and fixed-to-mobile calls makes it about $26.40/month inc GST. A Click2Call Cloud PBX User is $25/month ex GST (about $27.50 inc GST) and includes a local number, 300 outbound minutes, unlimited inbound calls, and AI call recording and transcription. On a like-for-like calling bundle they're within about a dollar a month. The real difference is that Click2Call has no lock-in contract, no separate network arrangement and no equipment rental, with self-serve setup.
Can I keep my Telstra phone number?
Yes. Porting is regulated in Australia and Telstra must support it. Click2Call ports local (02/03/07/08), mobile, and 1300/1800 numbers from Telstra. Local or mobile porting is $50 ex GST; 1300/1800 is $100 ex GST. Your number stays live with Telstra during the port, so there's no downtime, and it typically completes in 5–10 business days.
Do I have to cancel my Telstra contract first?
Check your TIPT agreement first. Per Telstra's TIPT Critical Information Summary (July 2026), licences carry a 12-month minimum term and rental equipment a 36-month term, with an early termination charge (30% of remaining minimum monthly charges plus outstanding hardware) to leave early. Many businesses run Click2Call alongside their existing service and port once ready, or wait until the Telstra term ends. Click2Call itself has no lock-in.
What's the difference between TIPT and Click2Call?
Both are cloud phone systems. TIPT is a per-user licensed Telstra platform (Basic/Standard/Executive packs) sold through a Telstra Business Technology Centre, requiring you to arrange your own network, with 12-month licences and 36-month equipment rental. Click2Call is a self-serve Australian Cloud PBX: sign up online, use any internet, bring your own device or the app, flat $25/user/month ex GST, no lock-in — with AI call recording, transcription and an AI receptionist included.
