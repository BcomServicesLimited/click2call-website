VoIP for Small Business Australia: The Complete Setup Guide

A step-by-step guide for Australian small businesses on how to switch to a VoIP phone system, from choosing a provider to setting up your hardware.

By Royce Clark • Published on 19 Feb 2026

Switching your small business to a VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) phone system is one of the most impactful upgrades you can make. It cuts costs, increases flexibility, and provides a suite of professional features that were once only available to large enterprises. This guide will walk you through the entire process, step-by-step, for a smooth transition.

Step 1: Assess Your Needs and Internet Connection

Before you start, take stock of your requirements:

  • How many users do you have? This will determine the number of licenses you need.
  • What features are essential? Do you need call recording, an auto-attendant (IVR), or CRM integration? Make a list of must-haves and nice-to-haves.
  • Do you want to keep your existing phone number? Most providers offer number porting.

Most importantly, check your internet connection. VoIP relies on a stable, high-quality internet connection. Run a speed test to check your upload and download speeds and latency (ping). For reliable call quality, a business-grade NBN connection is strongly recommended.

Step 2: Choose the Right VoIP Provider

Not all VoIP providers are created equal. Look for an Australian-based provider that offers:

  • Transparent Pricing: Clear per-user, per-month pricing with no hidden fees. Check what's included in the monthly fee (e.g., standard calls, support).
  • Local Support: Having access to an Australian support team who understands the local market is invaluable.
  • Scalability: Choose a provider that makes it easy to add or remove users as your business grows or changes.
  • A Strong Feature Set: Ensure the provider offers the features you identified in Step 1. A provider like Click2Call includes advanced features like AI-powered transcription and an AI Receptionist even in affordable plans.

Step 3: Select Your Hardware (or Go App-Only)

One of the biggest benefits of VoIP is that you don't necessarily need traditional desk phones.

  • Softphones (Apps): Most providers offer free desktop and mobile apps that turn your computer or smartphone into a fully-featured business phone. This is the most flexible and cost-effective option, perfect for remote teams.
  • VoIP Desk Phones: If you prefer a physical handset, you'll need to purchase VoIP-compatible phones. Brands like Yealink and Grandstream are popular choices. Your provider can usually supply pre-configured phones for a plug-and-play experience.
  • Headsets: A good quality USB or Bluetooth headset is essential for clear, hands-free communication, especially when using softphones.

Step 4: The Onboarding and Porting Process

Once you've signed up with a provider, the setup process begins:

  1. Configuration: Your provider will work with you to configure your system. This includes setting up your auto-attendant ("Press 1 for Sales..."), voicemail, call groups, and business hours.
  2. Number Porting: You will need to sign a Porting Authority Form (PAF) to give your new provider permission to move your phone number from your old carrier. This process is managed entirely by the provider and can take 5-30 business days. Do not cancel your old service until porting is complete.
  3. Hardware Delivery: If you ordered desk phones, they will be shipped to you.

Step 5: Go-Live and Training

On the scheduled "go-live" day, your number will be active on the new system. Here’s what to do:

  • Plug in your phones: If you have desk phones, simply plug them into your office network. They should automatically connect to the system.
  • Install the apps: Download the softphone apps onto your computers and mobiles and log in with the credentials provided.
  • Test everything: Make some inbound and outbound calls to ensure everything is working as expected.
  • Train your team: While modern VoIP systems are intuitive, a quick training session on how to use the new features (like call transfer, conferencing, and checking voicemail) will ensure a smooth adoption.

Conclusion

Making the switch to VoIP is a straightforward process that delivers immediate benefits for Australian small businesses. By choosing a reputable local provider and following these steps, you can unlock a more powerful, professional, and cost-effective communication system that will grow with your business.
