How Much Does a Business Phone System Cost in Australia? (2026 Guide)

A detailed breakdown of the costs involved in a business phone system in Australia, including hardware, licensing, call rates, and the total cost of ownership.

By Royce Clark • Published on 22 Feb 2026

Budgeting for a new business phone system can be confusing. With so many options available, from traditional on-premise hardware to modern cloud-based solutions, understanding the true cost is essential. This guide provides a transparent breakdown of what Australian businesses can expect to pay for a phone system in 2026, helping you make an informed decision and avoid hidden fees.

The Old Way: Traditional PBX Costs

An on-premise PBX system involves a significant upfront investment. While these systems are becoming less common, it's important to understand their cost structure as a baseline.

  • Hardware: The physical PBX unit itself can cost anywhere from $5,000 to over $20,000, depending on the size of your business.
  • Handsets: Desk phones can range from $100 for a basic model to over $500 for an executive video phone. For a 10-person office, this adds $1,000 - $5,000.
  • Installation: Professional installation and configuration can cost between $1,000 and $5,000.
  • Line Rental: You'll pay ongoing monthly fees for your physical phone lines (ISDN or PSTN), which can be several hundred dollars per month.
  • Maintenance: A support contract can cost 15-20% of the initial hardware cost, per year.

The total upfront cost for a 10-person business can easily exceed $10,000, with significant ongoing fees.

The New Way: Cloud PBX Pricing

Cloud-based phone systems, like Click2Call, operate on a much simpler and more transparent subscription model. This shifts the cost from a large capital expenditure (CapEx) to a predictable operating expenditure (OpEx).

The primary cost is a per-user, per-month license fee. This fee typically includes everything you need to run your phone system.

What's Included in a Cloud PBX Plan?

With a provider like Click2Call, your monthly fee covers:

  • License to use the platform: Access to all the features of your chosen plan.
  • Unlimited Calls: Most plans include unlimited standard calls to Australian local, national, and mobile numbers (subject to a fair use policy).
  • Free Software: Use of the mobile app (iOS/Android) and desktop softphone app.
  • Support & Maintenance: All support, software updates, and system maintenance are handled by the provider.
  • Number Porting: The one-time cost of moving your existing phone number to the new service is usually included.

Other Potential Costs

  • Handsets: While not required (you can use the free apps), if you want physical desk phones, you'll need to purchase VoIP-compatible models. These typically cost $150 - $400 per phone.
  • Call Rates: Calls to international numbers, 13/1300 numbers, and other premium services are charged on a per-minute basis. These rates are generally much lower than traditional carriers, starting from $0.10/min (ex GST) for Australian and NZ fixed calls.
  • Direct Numbers (DDIs): A dedicated Direct Dial Number (DDI) costs $5.00 per month (ex GST) with Click2Call.
  • Toll-Free Numbers: 1800 Toll-Free numbers are available — contact Click2Call for current pricing.

For a 10-person business, moving to a Cloud PBX like Click2Call is significantly more cost-effective than a traditional system.
