How Does VoIP Work Over Melbourne's NBN Connection?

Most Melbourne businesses are now connected to the NBN, and a VoIP phone system Melbourne-wide works well over any standard NBN connection. Each active call uses approximately 100 Kbps of bandwidth in each direction. A typical NBN 25 plan — the most common residential and small business tier — can comfortably support 10 or more simultaneous calls alongside normal internet use.

If your Melbourne office is in a building with a fibre-to-the-premises (FTTP) connection, you will have more than enough bandwidth for any realistic call volume. Businesses in older buildings using fibre-to-the-node (FTTN) connections can also run VoIP without issues, provided the connection is stable. The main thing to watch for is not speed but consistency — a connection that drops out or fluctuates heavily will affect call quality more than a slow but stable one.

For businesses in Melbourne's CBD or inner suburbs where building internet connections can be shared across multiple tenants, it is worth checking whether your connection has quality-of-service (QoS) settings that can prioritise voice traffic. Click2Call's support team can advise on this if needed. For most standard office setups in suburbs like South Yarra, Carlton, or Docklands, the default NBN connection will work without any special configuration.

One practical advantage of VoIP over NBN is that your phone number is no longer tied to your physical address. If your Melbourne business moves — from a Richmond co-working space to a permanent office in Prahran, for example — your phone number moves with you. There is no need to notify customers of a new number, no waiting for a line transfer, and no downtime.
