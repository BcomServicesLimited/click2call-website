Teams Is Great — But It Can’t Call Phones Yet

Microsoft Teams is great for chatting with your team, sharing files, and jumping on a video call. But out of the box it can’t do one thing — call a regular phone number. You can’t ring a customer’s mobile, call a supplier, or receive a call from someone dialling your business number.

Click2Call fixes that. We connect your Teams account to the real phone network so your team can call and receive calls from any number in Australia — directly inside the Teams app they already use every day. Nothing changes for them except a dial pad appears.

How It Works

Three things connect together. That’s it.

1. Your Teams App

Your team keeps using Teams exactly as they do now. A dial pad appears and they can make and receive calls like a normal phone.

2. Click2Call Connects Them

We link your Teams account to our Australian phone network. This is the bridge that lets Teams talk to real phone numbers.

3. Any Phone Number in Australia

Once connected, your team can call any landline or mobile in Australia — and customers can call your business number and reach them in Teams.

This approach — called Direct Routing — is Microsoft-approved. It replaces Microsoft's Domestic Calling Plan (which isn't available at an affordable price in Australia) with a local provider like Click2Call.

Before you start: You need a Microsoft 365 Business plan that includes Microsoft Teams (for example, Microsoft 365 Business Basic, Standard, or Premium). A personal Microsoft 365 subscription will not work — Teams calling requires a business plan. If your team already uses Teams for chat and meetings, you almost certainly have this already.
