What Is Microsoft Teams Calling?

Microsoft Teams is a fantastic tool for internal collaboration—chat, file sharing, and video meetings. By default, however, it can't connect to the outside world's telephone network. You can't use it to call a customer's mobile, a supplier's landline, or receive a call from a new sales lead.

Microsoft Teams Calling solves this problem. It bridges the gap by connecting your Teams environment to the Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN). This upgrade transforms Teams from just an internal collaboration tool into a fully-featured business phone system (PBX), allowing users to make and receive external calls directly from the Teams interface they already know and use every day.

How Does Click2Call Add Phone Calls to Teams?

We use a Microsoft-approved method called Direct Routing to connect your Teams account to our Australian voice network.

1. Your Microsoft Teams

Your users continue to use the standard Teams app on their computer or mobile. Nothing changes for them.

2. Click2Call Direct Routing

We configure a secure link between your Microsoft 365 tenant and our certified Session Border Controller (SBC).

3. Public Phone Network

Our network handles the connection to the outside world, allowing calls to flow to and from any landline or mobile number.

This is a more flexible and cost-effective approach than Microsoft's own 'Calling Plans', as it gives you the power of a dedicated voice specialist (us!) managing your telephony.

Microsoft Teams Direct Routing is a method of connecting Microsoft Teams to the Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN) via a certified Session Border Controller (SBC) and a third-party telephony provider like Click2Call. This allows Teams users to make and receive standard phone calls to any number in Australia and internationally, directly within the Teams app.

Yes. To use Direct Routing, each user must have a Microsoft 365 licence that includes the Phone System add-on. Click2Call provides the SIP trunks and Australian phone numbers; Microsoft provides the Phone System software licence. We can advise on the most cost-effective licence combination for your team.

Microsoft Calling Plans are Microsoft's own calling service, which can be expensive and are not always available in all Australian regions. Direct Routing uses a third-party provider like Click2Call to supply the calling capability, which is typically 40–60% cheaper than Microsoft Calling Plans and offers more flexibility, including Australian local numbers and 1300 numbers.
