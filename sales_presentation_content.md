## Slide 2 — Why We Changed the Pricing

**Heading:** The Old Pricing Was Costing Us Deals

**Body:**
Our previous pricing model required prospects to understand multiple components: number rental, call rates per minute, channel fees, and plan tiers. Research consistently shows that pricing complexity is one of the top reasons B2B prospects disengage before converting.

**Key points:**
- Prospects were leaving the pricing page without enquiring
- Sales calls spent too long explaining the model rather than closing
- Competitors with simpler pricing were winning on perceived value
- Internal feedback: "too many questions about what the total cost actually is"

**Insight:** Simplicity is a competitive advantage. When a prospect can calculate their monthly cost in 10 seconds, the conversation moves to value — not confusion.

## Slide 4 — What "300 Minutes" Actually Means

**Heading:** 300 Minutes Covers the Average Australian Business User Comfortably

**Body:**
300 minutes of outbound calling per month works out to 15 minutes per business day. Research from telecommunications industry bodies shows the average Australian office worker makes between 8 and 12 minutes of outbound calls per day. Our fair use cap sits well above this average.

**Key talking points for sales:**
- 300 minutes = 5 hours of outbound calls per month
- Average business user makes 8–12 minutes of outbound calls per day
- For a 5-day working week across 4 weeks, that is 160–240 minutes — well under the cap
- The cap is designed to prevent abuse (call centres, telemarketers), not to penalise normal businesses
- Inbound calls are completely unlimited — no cap, no charge

**Objection pre-empt:** "What if we go over?" — Usage above 300 minutes is charged at standard pay-as-you-go rates. This is disclosed in our Terms & Conditions (Section 9). For high-volume users, a SIP Trunk at $50/month with 500 minutes may be a better fit.

## Slide 6 — How to Present This in a Sales Call

**Heading:** Lead With the Monthly Total, Not the Per-User Rate

**Body:**
The most effective way to present our pricing is to ask the prospect how many users they need, then give them a single monthly number immediately.

**Recommended sales script:**

> "How many people need a phone line? Five? Great — that's $125 plus GST per month. That includes everyone's local number, all your inbound calls, and 300 minutes of outbound per user. No setup fees, no lock-in contract, and you can cancel any time."

**Why this works:**
- Anchors the conversation on a concrete, easy-to-remember number
- Eliminates the "I need to work out the total" objection
- Positions us as transparent and trustworthy from the first interaction
- Creates an immediate comparison point against their current bill

**Follow-up:** "What are you paying now?" — This opens the door to a cost comparison that almost always favours Click2Call.

## Slide 8 — Handling the Most Common Objections

**Heading:** Every Objection Has a Simple, Honest Answer

**Body:** These are the four objections you will hear most often, and the recommended responses.

**Objection 1: "Is it really unlimited?"**
Response: "Inbound calls are completely unlimited. Outbound calls include 300 minutes per user per month, which covers the average business user with room to spare. If you make a lot of outbound calls, we can look at a SIP Trunk option which gives you 500 minutes per trunk."

**Objection 2: "What happens if we go over 300 minutes?"**
Response: "Any usage above 300 minutes is charged at standard rates, which are disclosed in our terms. Most businesses never hit the cap — it's there to prevent misuse, not to penalise normal calling."

**Objection 3: "We're with Telstra — why would we switch?"**
Response: "Most of our customers came from Telstra. The main reasons are cost — you're likely paying more for fewer features — and flexibility. We have no lock-in contracts, so you can try us risk-free."

**Objection 4: "We need to think about it."**
Response: "Of course. Can I ask what the main question is? If it's about cost, I can send you a side-by-side comparison with what you're paying now. If it's about features, I can walk you through a quick demo."

## Slide 9 — The Fair Use Policy — What to Say and What Not to Say

**Heading:** Be Transparent About the Fair Use Policy — It Builds Trust

**Body:**
Our marketing advertises "unlimited calling (fair use policy applies)." The 300-minute cap is documented in our Terms & Conditions (Section 9). Here is how to handle this in a sales context.

**What to say:**
- "We advertise unlimited calling with a fair use policy. The policy caps outbound calls at 300 minutes per user per month, which is well above average business usage."
- "The cap is there to prevent misuse by call centres and telemarketers — it doesn't affect normal business calling."
- "Inbound calls are genuinely unlimited with no cap at all."

**What not to say:**
- Do not describe it as "unlimited" without mentioning the fair use policy
- Do not promise that the cap will never apply to their business
- Do not discourage prospects from reading the terms

**Why transparency wins:** Customers who feel they were misled churn faster and leave negative reviews. Customers who understood what they were buying from day one become long-term advocates.
