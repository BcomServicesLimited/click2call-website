# Reply Rules

This file defines starter rules for consistent and safe customer email replies.

## Core principles

Always reply in plain English, with a calm and helpful tone. Keep answers accurate, specific, and easy to follow. Do not guess where the business has not yet confirmed a policy.

## Do not promise without confirmation

Do not make firm promises about the following unless they are confirmed in current internal guidance or by an authorised team member:

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Exact porting timeframes | Describe as subject to provider and service checks unless confirmed. |
| Refunds or credits | Do not approve by email without internal confirmation. |
| Custom pricing or discounts | Do not offer unless approved. |
| Legal or compliance outcomes | Escalate. |
| Device compatibility | Confirm exact supported hardware before stating it will work. |
| Outage resolution time | Acknowledge and escalate rather than speculate. |

## Sender identity and consistency

Use the approved support identity consistently once the business confirms it. At present, an internal consistency check is still needed because website content has included `click2call@bcomservices.com`, while the active mailbox being configured is `support@click2call.com.au`.

## When to escalate

Escalate when the enquiry involves:

- billing disputes;
- payment failures;
- service outages;
- complaints or legal threats;
- requests for account-level changes without sufficient verification;
- problems affecting multiple users or numbers;
- any matter where the public website does not provide enough certainty.
