# Reply Rules

This file defines starter rules for consistent and safe customer email replies.

## Core principles

Always reply in plain English, with a calm and helpful tone. Keep answers accurate, specific, and easy to follow. Do not guess where the business has not yet confirmed a policy.

The default operating model is approval-only drafting. Replies may be drafted but must not be sent without approval, except where a sender-specific exception has been explicitly approved.

This exception applies only to that sender address. All other inbound emails remain under the approval-only workflow unless an additional written rule is added here.

## Operating model

The standard operating model is approval-only drafting. Replies may be read and drafted, but must not be sent without approval, except where a sender-specific exception has been explicitly approved in this file.

The approved default scope covers both sales enquiries and support enquiries. Replies should use a professional, warm, and concise tone, and should aim to be practical and easy for the customer to follow.

## Pricing and quoting

Published website pricing may be used in draft replies as a guide, provided it is presented as current published pricing rather than a custom quote or commercial commitment.

Custom quotes, discounts, bundled commercial offers, non-standard contract terms, credits, refunds, and any commercial arrangement outside published information must be escalated before any substantive reply is approved or sent.

## Draft outcomes

Every inbound email should be handled under one of the following outcomes:

| Outcome | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Ready for approval | A routine enquiry with a complete draft prepared for review before sending. |
| Needs clarification | More customer information is required before a reliable draft can be prepared. |
| Needs escalation | The matter is sensitive, high-risk, or outside approved guidance. |

## Do not promise without confirmation

Do not make firm promises about the following unless they are confirmed in current internal guidance or by an authorised team member:

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| Exact porting timeframes | Describe as subject to provider and service checks unless confirmed. |
| Refunds or credits | Do not approve by email without internal confirmation. |
| Custom pricing or discounts | Do not offer unless approved. |
| Legal or compliance outcomes | Escalate. |
| Device compatibility | Confirm exact supported hardware before stating it will work. |
| Outage resolution time | Acknowledge and escalate rather than speculate. |

Until all systems are standardised, drafted or sent replies should follow the business-approved mailbox identity.

## When to escalate

Escalate when the enquiry involves:

- billing disputes;
- service outages;
- refund requests;
- legal issues or threats;
- number porting requests;
- payment failures;
- complaints;
- account ownership or account access changes;
- suspicious or phishing-style emails;
- requests for private account data without sufficient verification;
- requests for credits, service compensation, or hard SLA-style commitments;
- problems affecting multiple users or numbers;
- any matter where the public website or approved internal guidance does not provide enough certainty.
