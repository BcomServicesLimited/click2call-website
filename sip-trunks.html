What Is a SIP Trunk?

A SIP Trunk is the modern, digital equivalent of a traditional telephone line. Instead of a physical copper wire running to your building, a SIP trunk uses your business internet connection to link your phone system (PBX) to the public telephone network. 'SIP' stands for Session Initiation Protocol, which is the open-standard technology that establishes and manages voice calls over the internet (VoIP).

Replacing ISDN and Analogue Lines

For decades, businesses relied on ISDN (Integrated Services Digital Network) or older analogue PSTN lines to make and receive calls. These technologies are now obsolete and being phased out across Australia as part of the NBN rollout. A SIP trunk is the direct replacement. It provides the same functionality—making and receiving multiple calls at once—but does so more efficiently and cost-effectively over your data connection. Each 'channel' in a SIP trunk allows for one concurrent call, so a 10-channel SIP trunk is the equivalent of 10 traditional phone lines.
